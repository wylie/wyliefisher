Bringing decades of illustration work to the web

Mark S. Fisher is an illustrator whose work spans editorial illustration, advertising, publishing, and personal projects. His artwork combines technical precision, storytelling, and a playful imagination that often leans toward science fiction, retro-futurism, and whimsical visual narratives.

The goal of this project was to create a portfolio site that allows the artwork to take center stage while remaining easy to browse across desktop and mobile devices.

Rather than competing with the illustrations, the design focuses on simplicity, clear navigation, and large imagery that highlights the detail and personality of the work itself.

What I worked on

* Site architecture
* Portfolio organization
* Responsive layouts
* Image presentation and gallery design
* Accessibility improvements
* Performance optimization
* Mobile-friendly navigation

Technologies

Astro · TypeScript · Responsive Design · Accessibility

Why it matters

This project was especially meaningful because it gave me the opportunity to help my father share his creative work online. The result is a site that feels true to his artistic style while making decades of illustration work easier to discover and explore.
